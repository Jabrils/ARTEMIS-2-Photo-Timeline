# Finding: Photo Navigation Gap in Mission Progress Bar

**Type**: Gap
**Severity**: Low
**File**: `src/hud/ProgressBar.tsx`

---

## What Was Found

The Mission Progress bar renders clickable orange dots for photo milestones, but provides no sequential prev/next navigation controls. Users must visually locate and click individual dots to jump between photo positions. The ~13 photo milestones clustered between T+120h and T+140h are tightly packed on the bar and difficult to click individually.

There is no mechanism to step through photos one at a time and move Orion's 3D position to match.

## Scope

- `src/hud/ProgressBar.tsx` — progress bar UI, milestone dot rendering
- `src/store/mission-store.ts` — `setSimTime`, `setTimeMode` (already supports time jumping)
- `src/data/mission-config.ts` — `MILESTONES` array with `photo` field

## Preliminary Assessment

No architectural change required. `setSimTime` + `setTimeMode('sim')` already support arbitrary time jumps. Missing capability is purely UI: two arrow buttons that filter `milestoneData` to photo-only entries, compute the adjacent photo index relative to current elapsed time, and call `setSimTime`.

## Evidence

- `src/hud/ProgressBar.tsx:98-101` — existing click handler calls `setSimTime`; no prev/next wiring exists
- `src/data/mission-config.ts:29-44` — 15 milestones, all with `photo` field set (photo-only MILESTONES array in this config)
- `photo-manifest.json` — 19 entries including 3 new photos not yet in `mission-config.ts`
